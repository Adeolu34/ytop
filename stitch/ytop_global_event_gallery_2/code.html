Purpose Is Built, Not Found

Sarah Adebayo

Published on Oct 24, 2023 • 5 min read

Share

We often speak of purpose as if it were a hidden treasure buried somewhere in the desert of our lives, waiting for us to stumble upon it with a worn-out map. But the truth is far more empowering: purpose isn't found; it is built, brick by brick, through our actions and choices.

The Myth of the "Lightning Bolt" Moment

Many young leaders wait for a sudden epiphany, a moment where the clouds part and their destiny is revealed. While some may experience this, for most of us, clarity comes from engagement. At YTOP Global, we've seen thousands of youths discover their path not by thinking, but by doing.

When you volunteer for a community cleanup, mentor a junior student, or organize a small event, you are gathering data about yourself. You learn what frustrates you, what energizes you, and where your skills can solve real problems.

"Action is the foundational key to all success. Don't wait for perfect clarity before you begin. Start walking, and the path will appear beneath your feet."

Building Your Purpose Architecture

Think of purpose as architecture. You need a foundation (values), pillars (skills), and a roof (impact).

  • Values: What do you stand for? Integrity, compassion, innovation?
  • Skills: What are you good at? Or what are you willing to become good at?
  • Impact: Who benefits from your work? Purpose is rarely about self; it's about service.

The journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. But it continues with resilience. Building purpose is hard work. It requires showing up when you don't feel like it. It requires navigating failure not as a stop sign, but as a detour sign pointing you in a better direction.

Join the Builders

You don't have to build alone. That's why communities like YTOP exist. To provide the mortar between your bricks—the support, mentorship, and network you need to construct a life of meaning. Look around you. The tools are in your hands. Start building today.
